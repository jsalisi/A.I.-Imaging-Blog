Retrospective #1: Sticks and Balls

18 Sept 2018, by: Alex Phan

In my IT Service and Project Management course, we played with sticks and balls. In groups of 5, our goal was to build the highest tower possible with the sticks and balls in a set amount of time. My team consist of Trevor, Travis, Justin, Bhavya, and myself. We did this activity twice. The first time we did this activity, we didn’t plan out a strategy. We just started build our tower as fast as possible without thinking. I think, several members of our team were confused of what everybody was doing. Even though, we ended up with the highest tower, it was a mess. We just winged it, and we didn’t work together as team. The tallest person did majority of the work, while the rest of the team members just stood there watching. The second time we did this activity, we had half the amount of time. This time we spent the first few minutes planning. We had a strategy that everybody understood, and everybody had a task to complete. We decided to use triangle strategy because the triangle is the strongest shape. The result was that, we had the second highest tower. Although our tower wasn’t the highest, we communicated and worked as a team to complete this activity. From this activity I learned three things. To have a successful team, we need communication, team work, and time management. When everyone has a role and know what to do, the team can be more efficient and complete tasks faster.
