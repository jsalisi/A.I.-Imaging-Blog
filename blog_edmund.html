Retrospective #1

18 Sept 2018, by: Edmund Yao

Balls and Sticks

The experience with the balls and sticks was a refreshing learning experience. Not often does a computing student get to play with kids toys. With the first exercise, it was really cool to see the team come up with the same triangular-prism-like architecture right off the bat. Because everyone was on the same page, we were able to get right into the build. Initially, we split up the team of 6 equally into three triangle builders and three stackers however, after a couple triangles were made, we quickly realized more manpower was needed for the stacking, so the team adjusted accordingly. Once at the final stages, it became clear that we didn’t have enough pieces for our roof and so we winged it; that was not a good idea. The design was not the best… With the remaining minutes we came together and discussed a more fitting roof of a winning tower and because of that, we won. With the second exercise, having less overall time but having a planning phase meant properly mapping out a sequence, but at the cost of less time. I felt like it was not a good trade-off especially when we had the carry over experience of the previous build. I felt like the overall ordeal was probably a lot more hectic. The loss of build quality caused our tower to topple over even though the design was exactly the same as before.

Blind Drawing

Explaining a drawing with a ton of interlocking elements without saying what the picture is, is extremely difficult. I am a person who prefers explaining with visuals, and when that was taken away from me, its not a good time. Having no other teammates to help articulate the picture, emphasized the struggle. Having a team to cover a member’s short comings is not always what one thinks about when working in teams, so this exercise was a pleasant reminder of so.

Mission Statement

For researchers who want to be able to obtain and compare data from scans of human body parts.
PyScan3D, a software provides a means of extracting data from 3d scans and allows transfer of said data to be stored in a database.
Unlike all other competitors ours software is specifically for the use of building BCIT’s human body part database.
