Building a tower

18 Sept 2018, by: Justin Salisi

Today, we built a tower (using sticks and balls) in our project groups. Initially, we were given a time limitation of 15 minutes to plan, build and test our design.

The test consisted of 2 requirements; The tower must:
1.) Stand by itself
2.) Last for 60 seconds

We gathered our ideas within the first few minutes and decided to build our tower based on the triangle; a shape that was known for its durability. Due to the nature of starting a new task, each of us were pitching our ideas as to how we were going to build it. Being the leader of our group, I decided to use the simplest design so we could finish building and testing within the time limit.When it was time for testing, ours held up the longest and eventually won the competition! We were then given another assignment before the next competition: divide all the resources evenly among every participating team.We were given 5 minutes for this task and it proved to be a simple task in the end.

The next event we repeated the same process as the last but were given 2 minutes for preparation and 7 minutes for the build process. I outlined simple tasks for my team to complete to speed up our build process; this time only taking 4 minutes to complete. We were able to last for another 60 seconds but another team had several more pieces than us which allowed them to win.

During these events, I learned a lot about my leadership capabilites. I am able to concisely delegate tasks, command the respect of my team, and listen to thoughts and suggestions of others. However, my inability to coordinate with other leaders was what ultimately led us to have unequal resources for each group; eventually costing us a win.
