Retrospective #1

18 Sept 2018, by: Travis Chan

I learned a couple of lessons during the class that involved building with sticks and balls. One of the lessons were that when communicating with the project leader, all other members should wait and take turns, so the leader doesn’t get lost in chaos. Without good communication between members, the project will not go smooth because requirements won’t be clear, and everyone will be waiting for the questions to be answered. In my opinion the take away from this activity is time management because we had to build a similar tower with half the time as the first. Dividing up tasks were helpful in saving time because during the first time we built the tower all of us just started to build in our own way. After we split the task up we were able to build the tower faster, but we didn’t win in the end. An additional lesson I learned was that planning is a helpful way of keeping the group together. When you give a member a specific task instead of a vague idea of what to do, they can assess the work and are able to ask for help quicker than later.

Mission Statement

For researchers who want to be able to obtain and compare data from scans of human body parts.
PyScan3D, a software provides a means of extracting data from 3d scans and allows transfer of said data to be stored in a database.
Unlike all other competitors ours software is specifically for the use of building BCIT’s human body part database.
