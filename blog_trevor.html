Retrospective Week 3

18 Sept 2018, by: Trevor Redomond

For this week we were put into groups of 5 and were tasked with having to build the tallest tower. I was on a team with other members of my team for the project, with Alex Phan, Bhavya Shah, Justin Salisi and Travis Chan. With the connecting sticks and balls and 15 minutes to complete, my team immediately came to a consensus that we needed to build our tower out of triangles. I offered to keep time for my team and decided to set the timer with 1 minute less than what we were given. I did this so that if I forgot to check the time and the timer finished counting down, we would still have a minute to finalize our tower. This was just a strategy I thought of on the spot, unlike later in the day where we had a few planned strategies.

My team started building our tower at the bottom of the stairs in our area, and with 10 minutes left we finished, with our tower standing higher than anyone else. We then had to take it to the top of the stairs, for the final display. We split the tower into three parts and members of the team took them up to the top. I was keeping time and notifying my team every time a minute went down. We made it to the top and reassembled the tower with a few minutes to spare. Our final minutes of the challenge were spent making sure the tower was properly balanced. We ended up winning the first challenge, with the tallest tower and one of the only ones to not fall over.

The next challenge was to build the tallest tower, but this time with half the time and were given two minutes to plan. Our strategy in basing our structure around triangles was the same, but this time instead of building the tower at the bottom, we built the individual sections of the tower. When each individual part was finished, we took the parts up to the top and build the tower there. The tower we built the second time had a harder time staying up; the balance was off because of unproperly placed balls and sticks. We got our tower to stand up and it was the second tallest.

Looking over and comparing both challenges, I personally believed that we had a better structure in the first challenge, it was taller and balanced better. Despite having a worse tower during the second challenge, we completed the structure in the finished area much faster than the first challenge, after planning out how we were going to build and get the tower to the “finished” area.

I think the important thing about this activity is that it was an eye opener of how much faster tasks can be completed if you plan them, where I usually just jump in ‘head first’ without much thought process. Hopefully I will keep the skills and ideas I learned from this activity for the future so that I can properly plan in a team and deliver the best work possible.
